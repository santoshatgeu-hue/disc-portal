About this assessment This is a 28-item forced-choice DISC instrument based on the classic Everything DiSC® and Thomas PPA methodology. Each item presents 4 behavioural statements — one per dimension. You select the statement that is Most like how you naturally behave, and the statement that is Least like you. This generates a raw score, intensity score, and profile graph for all four dimensions.

How the forced-choice format works
MOST
Select the one statement from the group of 4 that best describes how you most naturally behave. There are no right or wrong answers.
LEAST
Then select the statement that is least like you. It must be different from your Most selection.
Sample item
I take charge and push for results, even if it rubs people the wrong way. MOST
I enjoy meeting new people and thrive in lively, social settings.
I prefer a steady pace and like to work in a harmonious environment. LEAST
I believe in doing things correctly the first time, even if it takes longer.

Scoring methodology

Step 1 — Raw score calculation

Each of the 28 items contains 4 behavioural statements, each mapped to one DISC dimension (D, I, S, or C). For every item:

SelectionPoints addedWhy
Most selection+1 to that dimensionYou actively identify with this trait
Least selection−1 to that dimensionYou actively disidentify with this trait
Unselected words0 pointsNeutral / ambiguous

After all 28 items, each dimension has a raw score ranging from −28 to +28. In practice, most scores fall between −14 and +14.

Step 2 — Intensity score (normalised to 0–100)

Raw scores are converted to a 0–100 intensity scale for easier interpretation and profile graphing:

Intensity = ((raw_score + 28) / 56) × 100

This maps −28 → 0 and +28 → 100. A score of 50 means neutral (equal Most and Least selections for that dimension).

Intensity bandMeaning
75–100Strong / dominant tendency in this dimension
55–74Above average presence of this dimension
45–54Moderate — neither high nor low
25–44Below average — this dimension is less natural
0–24Low — this dimension is actively resisted

Step 3 — Primary style identification

The dimension with the highest intensity score is the primary DISC style. When two dimensions are within 5 points of each other, a blended style is reported (e.g. D/I, S/C).

Most people have 1 dominant style and 1–2 secondary elevated styles. Pure single-style profiles are less common.

Step 4 — Profile graph

The four intensity scores are plotted as a bar graph. The pattern of highs and lows — not just the single highest bar — reveals the full behavioral style. For example:

Reliability & validity notes

Forced-choice formats reduce social desirability bias compared to Likert-scale instruments because respondents cannot give every item a high rating. The ipsative scoring (Most +1, Least −1) creates a relative ranking of behavioral preferences rather than an absolute measure.

This instrument is designed for self-awareness and team development, not selection or clinical diagnosis. Results should be interpreted in context and verified through behavioural observation.

Test-retest reliability is highest when respondents answer based on their natural style rather than how they behave in a specific role or context.
